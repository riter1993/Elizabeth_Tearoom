Hi, I’m Felipe, a travel enthusiast, food lover, and the creator of Elizabeth Tearoom. Please enjoy my journey of how I share my love and passion with others.

Growing up with a mum who worked as a chef and a dad who was always grilling at our neighborhood barbeque parties, some of my fondest memories surround food.

After moving to London and finding myself living and cooking for myself, I wanted to recreate the recipes of my childhood to make me feel closer to home. Not surprisingly, I wasn’t alone. All my friends and roommates did the same. Sharing your food is sharing your life and your memories.

After trying so many different tastes and cuisines, learning how to make them was the next step. I would ask my friends to teach me how to cook their favorite meals, and every time I traveled somewhere new, I’d take a cooking or baking class which was my souvenir for the trip. I started volunteering to teach cupcake baking classes for children in East London and that’s when I saw how strangers can easily come together and create a closer bond through food.

When I moved back to Japan, I missed the variety and easy access to baked goods in Europe. I quickly realized that there was a huge opportunity to share my experiences and stories with people in my country, and make them feel closer to me.

A good friend of mine once told me that my love language is food.. and she was not wrong. I guess we forget how we ALL use food to express love: eating dinner as a family, waiting for someone to get home to eat together, bringing comfort food to those having a hard time, bringing baked goods into the office, buying your broken-hearted friend ice cream, buying that friend who lives far away a treat and having it delivered on a special date, or even sneaking some scraps to your pets... that is all love. We all love through food.
